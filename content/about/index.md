## What We Cover

Our newsroom runs four desks, each tracking a distinct territory of robotics:

**Humanoid** — Bipedal and general-purpose humanoid platforms: locomotion,
manipulation, embodiment strategies, and the industrial race to make
human-shaped machines useful.

**Industrial** — Manufacturing automation, collaborative robots, components,
and the systems that put robots to work: arms, grippers, motion control,
safety standards.

**Research** — What laboratories publish before industry productizes it:
new mechanisms, learning methods, materials, and the experiments that hint
at the next decade of machines.

**Mobility** — Machines that move through the world: autonomous vehicles,
drones, legged platforms, and delivery robots — and the regulations that
govern where they may go.

Alongside the news desks, our **Design** section publishes critique and
analysis: close readings of individual robots as designed objects, from
ASIMO to the latest humanoids.

## How We Read Robots

Most robotics media reports what happened. We ask a different question:
**why does this machine look and move the way it does?**

Every robot is the resolution of three forces:

- **Engineering** — what the machine must do, and the mechanisms that make it possible.
- **Morphology** — the structural logic of its body: proportion, articulation, the grammar of joints and mass.
- **Aesthetics** — the visual language of machines: what a robot's form communicates, and to whom.

Where these three intersect is where design decisions live — and that
intersection is our beat.

## How It's Made

Robotdesign.net is an **AI-native newsroom**. Articles are drafted by AI
reporters working from original sources; every factual claim must be
grounded in the source text, and every article links to it. The pipeline —
collection, verification, writing, publication — is designed, supervised,
and edited by a human editor. When the machine has nothing sourced to say,
it says nothing.

We believe this is worth stating plainly: the future of specialist media
will be built by small teams directing capable machines, and we are
building one in the open.
