Booster Robotics, a Chinese humanoid robot developer, has secured over $14 million in funding as the industry accelerates toward commercial deployment. The investment, detailed in an exclusive report by QQ News, underscores growing investor confidence in humanoid robots for industrial and service applications.

Booster Robotics focuses on developing full-sized humanoid robots designed for labor-intensive tasks such as warehouse logistics, manufacturing, and elder care. The company’s latest round of financing brings its total funding to over $30 million, positioning it among the leading startups in China’s humanoid robotics sector. The funds will be used to scale production, enhance robot dexterity and mobility, and expand deployment in real-world environments.

The humanoid robot market has seen a surge in investment globally, with companies like Tesla, Figure, and 1X raising significant capital. In China, government initiatives and private capital are fueling a wave of humanoid startups aiming to address labor shortages and automation demands. Booster Robotics’ robots are designed to operate alongside humans, performing tasks that require balance, manipulation, and navigation in unstructured settings.

The company’s flagship robot, the Booster T1, stands 1.7 meters tall and weighs 65 kg. It features 40 degrees of freedom, enabling complex movements such as walking, running, and object manipulation. The T1 uses onboard sensors and AI to autonomously plan motions and avoid obstacles. According to the company, the T1 has already been tested in logistics and manufacturing environments, showing capability in tasks like parcel sorting and assembly.

Commercial-scale production is a key challenge for humanoid robot companies. Booster Robotics plans to ramp up manufacturing capacity at its facility in Shenzhen, aiming to produce hundreds of units per year by 2025. The company employs a team of over 100 engineers specializing in mechanical design, control systems, and AI.

Analysts note that while humanoid robots have demonstrated progress in lab settings, widespread commercial adoption remains hindered by high costs, safety concerns, and the need for more reliable autonomy. Booster Robotics’ funding round, led by a consortium of venture capital firms and strategic investors, signals belief that these obstacles can be overcome within the next few years.

The latest investment follows a trend of Chinese robotics companies attracting global attention. In 2024, several Chinese humanoid startups raised capital, reflecting the country’s strong supply chain for hardware and components. Booster Robotics differentiates itself through a focus on affordability and practical utility, targeting price points that make humanoid robots accessible to medium-sized businesses.

As humanoid robots move from science fiction to commercial reality, Booster Robotics’ latest funding is a milestone for the industry. The company’s ability to deliver reliable, cost-effective robots will determine its success in capturing a share of the growing global market for human-like automation.
