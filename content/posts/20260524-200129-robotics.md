Fox News reports on a test conducted by an unnamed logistics company where humanoid robots worked continuously in a package handling operation. The test involved robots from a US-based humanoid robotics startup, demonstrating their ability to operate for extended periods without human intervention. The robots were tasked with picking, sorting, and moving packages in a simulated warehouse environment. According to the report, the robots maintained high throughput and accuracy over multiple shifts, with minimal downtime for maintenance or battery swaps. The startup's CEO highlighted that the robots are designed for 24/7 operation in logistics centers, offering a potential solution to labor shortages and the increasing demand for e-commerce fulfillment. The test results are expected to inform deployment decisions for several major logistics firms considering humanoid robots for warehouse automation. Fox News also noted that the robots utilize advanced perception and manipulation algorithms to handle a wide variety of package shapes and sizes. This marks a significant step in the commercial viability of humanoid robots in industrial settings, moving beyond research labs into real-world applications.
