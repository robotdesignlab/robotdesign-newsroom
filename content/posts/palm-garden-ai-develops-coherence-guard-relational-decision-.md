Palm Garden AI is developing Coherence Guard, a platform-agnostic relational decision layer for human-facing robots. The layer evaluates whether a robot's intended action is relationally coherent in real human environments, considering signals such as timing, proximity, boundary requests, emotional tone, trust preservation, and respectful withdrawal.

As general-purpose robots and humanoids evolve for hospitality, care, retail, education, guidance, and domestic environments, Coherence Guard aims to serve as an additional pre-action evaluation layer rather than replacing existing control stacks. Joachim Scheuerer, CEO of Palm Garden AI, explained that the layer assesses the difference between technically possible and socially appropriate actions.

> Before a robot executes an action, the layer can evaluate whether the action is **relationally coherent in a real human environment**.

Palm Garden AI has built its ANATTA 9 behavior infrastructure on the Transwarp Cloud Operating System (TCOS). Coherence Guard sits above or beside existing robot control, SDK/API, ROS 2, planning, or world-model systems. The company's Relational Infrastructure Framework (RIF) adds understanding of roles, intentions, vulnerabilities, and possible future consequences, enabling robots to withdraw if a person indicates discomfort. RIF is now available upon request.
