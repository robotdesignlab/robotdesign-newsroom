Researchers at the USC Viterbi School of Engineering have developed a robotic hand that can hear a melody once and play it back after just two minutes of self-taught practice on a keyboard, without relying on sheet music or preprogrammed scores. This advance could open new possibilities in medicine and therapy by enabling more adaptive and intuitive human-robot interaction.
