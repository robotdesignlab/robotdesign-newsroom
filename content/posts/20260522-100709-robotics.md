Josie Hughes, Assistant Professor at EPFL and head of the CREATE Lab, discussed using AI to generate novel designs for robotic manipulators. The talk focused on computational approaches to automate the design of robotic structures, reducing reliance on manual engineering intuition. Hughes highlighted techniques such as evolutionary algorithms and generative models to explore design spaces for manipulators that are optimized for specific tasks. She described work from her lab where AI produces designs that are often counter-intuitive yet highly efficient, outperforming traditionally designed manipulators in metrics like stiffness-to-weight ratio or dexterity. The research aims to accelerate prototyping by generating design candidates that engineers can then refine. Hughes emphasized the importance of incorporating physical constraints into the generative process to ensure manufacturability. The episode also covered challenges in bridging simulation and reality, and the potential for AI-designed robots to be more adaptable to niche applications.
