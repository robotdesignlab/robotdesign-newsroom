Generative Bionics this week unveiled Gene.01, a humanoid robot with full-body tactile sensing, at AMD Advancing AI 2026. The robot, built in six months, combines tactile skin with physics-native AI for industrial applications.

Gene.01 features full-body distributed tactile skin that detects touch, temperature, proximity, and force, enabling safer human-robot collaboration. The AI system integrates the robot's body, mechanics, and intelligence to better interpret and respond to the physical world.

> The robot features full-body distributed tactile skin that can detect touch, temperature, proximity, and force. This helps it anticipate human presence and respond **before and during contact**.

Generative Bionics is adapting the platform for its first industrial use case through a collaboration with Fincantieri, a global leader in shipbuilding, focused on shipyard operations. The company is also open-sourcing Gene.01's robot model to give physical AI developers a foundation for building industry-specific applications.
