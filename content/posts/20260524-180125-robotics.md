Beijing, China — The first high school humanoid robot soccer final is underway in Beijing, featuring eight schools competing with autonomous humanoid robots. The competition emphasizes coding and AI, as students program their robots to play soccer without remote control. The event, reported by CGTN, showcases the integration of robotics education in Chinese high schools, with teams developing strategies for locomotion, ball handling, and teamwork using humanoid platforms. The winners are determined by algorithm performance and match outcomes, highlighting technical skills in perception, planning, and control. This initiative aims to inspire youth interest in robotics and AI, aligning with China's push for STEM education and technological innovation.
