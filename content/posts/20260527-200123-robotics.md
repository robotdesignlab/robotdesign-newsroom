LEAD: IntBot, a humanoid robotics startup, has partnered with Certis Group, a Singapore-based security and facilities management firm, to deploy physical AI solutions across enterprise environments in Singapore. This collaboration marks a significant step in bringing humanoid robots from R&D into commercial service operations, leveraging Certis's extensive client network in security, healthcare, and logistics.

BACKGROUND: Certis Group has long provided integrated security and facilities management services, and has been expanding its robotics portfolio with service robots for patrolling, cleaning, and delivery. IntBot, founded in 2022, develops humanoid robots designed for physical tasks in structured environments. The partnership aims to combine IntBot's humanoid platform with Certis's operational expertise to address labor shortages and improve efficiency in sectors like healthcare, hospitality, and logistics. Competing platforms include Agility Robotics' Digit and Boston Dynamics' Atlas, but IntBot focuses on cost-effective, task-specific humanoids for indoor enterprise use.

KEY DETAILS: IntBot's humanoid robot features a bipedal design with lightweight actuators and a modular torso for task-specific customization. The robot stands approximately 1.7 meters tall and weighs 65 kg, with a payload capacity of 15 kg per arm. It uses a combination of vision-based navigation and force-torque sensing for stable locomotion and manipulation. The robot's walking speed is up to 1.5 m/s, and it can operate for 4 hours on a single charge. The partnership will initially deploy robots for security patrols and logistics tasks, such as carrying packages and monitoring premises. Certis will integrate IntBot's robots into its existing service ecosystem, providing maintenance and remote monitoring.

The design philosophy emphasizes safety and reliability in human-centric environments. IntBot uses a low-inertia actuator design to minimize impact forces during collisions, and the robot's exterior is padded with soft materials. The head features a simplified face with LED eyes for human-robot interaction, but the focus is on functional task execution rather than anthropomorphic aesthetics. The robots will be deployed in controlled indoor settings like office buildings and hospitals, where they can navigate autonomously using pre-mapped layouts and real-time obstacle avoidance.

OUTLOOK: The partnership positions IntBot and Certis to capture early enterprise adoption in Singapore, a market with strong government support for automation. Initial deployments are expected within 12 months, targeting security and logistics tasks. If successful, the collaboration could expand to other Asian markets, providing a blueprint for humanoid integration in service industries. However, challenges remain in cost reduction and proving reliability over long-term operations.
